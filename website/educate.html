Mental Health Information

What is a Mental Health Problem?

A mental health problem refers to conditions affecting thoughts, emotions, or behavior, causing distress and hindering daily life. Seeking help is crucial, as mental health problems are treatable, and support promotes recovery.

ADHD: Symptoms and Causes

ADHD, or Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. Genetic, neurological, and environmental factors contribute to its development.

Anxiety and Depression: Symptoms and Causes

Anxiety and depression are common mental health disorders with symptoms such as persistent sadness, worry, and changes in sleep patterns. Biological, genetic, and environmental factors contribute to their onset.

OCD: Symptoms and Causes

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) involves distressing thoughts and ritualistic behaviors. Imbalances in serotonin, genetic factors, and environmental stressors contribute to the development of OCD.

PTSD: Symptoms and Causes

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) develops after exposure to a traumatic event, leading to intrusive memories, avoidance, and changes in mood. Severity of trauma, lack of social support, and genetic factors increase the risk of PTSD.
